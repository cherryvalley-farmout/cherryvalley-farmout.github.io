Our Town

by Melissa Horvath & Doris Lange

May 14, 1974 — The Freeman’s Journal

❦

We are blessed and surrounded by an enthusiastic group of students at Cherry Valley Central School who need volunteers for the play OUR TOWN by Thornton Wilder, which will be presented in late May. They need boys’ dress clothes appropriate for the turn of the century, and possibly — particularly — a simple bridal gown. Can you help?

A letter arrived, and on the envelope the address was like this: Green Road Farm, Greer’s Corners, Otsego County, New Hampshire, United States of America, Western Hemisphere, The World, The Solar System, The Universe, The Mind of God.

Greer’s Corners is our town — in the Center of the World — and, as in the Universe, Our Town is about all our towns — all of us, all the time and all of life.

As you know, Cherry Valley was the bustling commercial center in 1814. It had a severe shortage of silver and copper — the banks and businesses had large bank notes but nothing to make change with. So, with typical ingenuity the Village printed $500 worth of paper “coins.” The May 1813 paper coin — a faithful record — a “Corporation Bill” — has been reclaimed by the Village Board as “Currency Scrip.”

❦

A Chunk of Natural History

Collectively we have lived in Upper New York State for 69 years, and we try to be observant and cognizant of things — occasionally when a chunk of natural history from other places crops up unexpectedly.

We have bamboo coming down the stream — dark all over, with red on its head, looking like a woodpecker. When it was described to a naturalist friend, we were astounded to learn it was a Barbed Warbler — a bird approximately 6 feet long and has a wing span of 6 feet. We thought it had blown up from the end of the Mississippi and hung around abandoned territories. The Great Blue Heron does this too, and the Green Herons that fish the creek also seem out of place much of the time.

Would you believe bamboo shooting up 2½ feet already? This year a flower garden where none had been before — who said that this climate doesn’t change? This is the climate. Another surprise: Have you seen the Mock Oranges which are large shrubs? Those Mock Oranges have not only survived NYS winters, but have also bred in the wild.

So, if one of you Farm Out readers passes a farm near Cherry Valley with green parrots squawking in a stand of bamboo 20 feet tall, know it can be believed.

Signs of the Season

There are many local and usual plants that grow with such explosive rapidity this time of year that they seem almost magic. Has anyone ever noticed how quickly Clover, Dandelions, or Asparagus grow within a 24-hour period?

Meanwhile we can enjoy cat tail sprouts and wait for the peas to come up. Have you tried tender young cat tails as a succulent vegetable?
