Farm Out

Cherry Valley Art Day

by Melissa Horvath & Doris Lange

May 29, 1974 — The Freeman’s Journal

❦

Hello, dear friends — is anybody reading? We’ve been having our Farm Out column for about four weeks now. It’s been wonderful to hear from readers, and we hope it will continue to be as much your column as ours. Do you like reading it?

❦

Art Day

Have you heard about Art Day? The fourth annual Cherry Valley Art Day will be held on Saturday, June 1st. The purpose of Art Day is to combine the many talents and the entertainment of the many talented people who live in this area.

This year’s Cherry Valley Art Day will feature a juried art show with work by Joseph Frasconi. The various facilities will be on display: tables, houses, auxiliary buildings, and hand equipment — all donated by local supporters for several years running. It promises to be a fine afternoon of art, crafts, and community.

❦

Important Stuff

1. Farm reports have been appearing in the Freeman-Leader of Otsego County, which has a million sparkling ideas for its readers in the June sun.

2. Has anyone noticed that the dock where Doris lives has produced a surprise this spring? Well, here’s a chicken story: we have heard from a neighbor that her flock — well-tended and productive all winter — decided the moment the warm weather arrived that the barn was entirely optional. The chickens are now roosting in the willows along the creek. They are, apparently, still laying — but the eggs have become a treasure hunt.

3. The dock, the wild onions, and the asparagus fern — not to mention the volunteer tomatoes from last year’s compost — are all coming in fast. It can be difficult to keep up with a farm in May.

❦

Good Luck

Good luck to all of you who are planting, building, fixing fences, chasing chickens, and otherwise trying to get ahead of the season. We are right there with you.

❦
